## Abstract
This letter addresses critical limitations in traditional fragility assessments used in randomized controlled trials, specifically those evaluating platelet-rich plasma for knee osteoarthritis. The author argues that the classic fragility index relies on assumptions regarding which group is modified, which can significantly skew estimates of evidence stability. As an alternative, the letter proposes a shift from P-value fragility toward evaluating relative risk deviation. It introduces the Relative Risk Index (RRI), calculated by dividing the difference between observed and expected values by the mean cell value. This approach aligns statistical evaluation more closely with clinical judgment by focusing on the required change to reach treatment neutrality rather than compatibility with a null hypothesis alone.
